Fried Chicken

Description

Fried chicken is made by first marinating chicken pieces in buttermilk or a mixture of yogurt, salt, pepper, garlic, and spices to tenderize and flavor the meat. After marinating for a few hours, the chicken is dredged in seasoned flour—often mixed with paprika, chili powder, and a pinch of baking powder for extra crispiness. Some recipes double-coat the chicken by dipping it back into buttermilk and flour again to create a thicker crust. The coated chicken is then deep-fried in hot oil (about 170–180°C) until golden brown and cooked through, usually 12–15 minutes depending on the size of the pieces. Once fried, it is placed on a wire rack or paper towel to drain excess oil, resulting in juicy, tender meat inside and a crispy, flavorful crust outside.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Oil
  • salt
  • Lemon
  • Spices
  • Fried Chicken Masala

Steps

  1. Wash the chicken pieces thoroughly and pat them dry with a clean towel.
  2. Marinate the chicken with salt, lemon juice, spices, and fried chicken masala. Mix well so every piece is evenly coated.
  3. Cover and let the chicken rest for at least 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  5. Carefully place the marinated chicken pieces into the hot oil.
  6. Fry the chicken until golden brown and fully cooked, turning occasionally for even frying.
  7. Remove the chicken and place it on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy your crispy fried chicken.

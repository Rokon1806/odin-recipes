Shwarma

Description

Shawarma is made by marinating thin slices of chicken or beef in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, paprika, and black pepper. The meat is traditionally stacked and slow-roasted on a vertical rotisserie, allowing it to cook evenly while staying juicy and flavorful. As it roasts, thin slices are shaved off for serving. The cooked meat is then wrapped in soft flatbread or pita with fresh vegetables like lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, and onions. It is usually topped with creamy garlic sauce, tahini, or chili sauce for extra flavor. The result is a warm, savory wrap that is both tender and richly spiced, perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Chicken or beef (thinly sliced)
  • Pita bread or flatbread
  • Yogurt
  • Lemon juice
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Cumin powder
  • Paprika or chili powder
  • Lettuce (shredded)
  • Cucumber (sliced)
  • Tomato (sliced)
  • Onion (sliced)
  • Garlic sauce or tahini sauce

Steps

  1. Wash and thinly slice the chicken or beef.
  2. In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, salt, black pepper, cumin, and paprika to prepare the marinade.
  3. Add the sliced meat to the marinade and mix well until fully coated.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to absorb the flavors.
  5. Heat a pan with a little oil over medium heat.
  6. Cook the marinated meat until fully done and slightly browned.
  7. Warm the pita bread or flatbread lightly on a pan.
  8. Place the cooked meat on the bread and add lettuce, cucumber, tomato, and onion.
  9. Drizzle garlic sauce or tahini sauce on top.
  10. Wrap the bread tightly, cut in half if desired, and serve warm.
